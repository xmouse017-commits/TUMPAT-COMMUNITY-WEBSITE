WHO WE ARE

A Melting Pot of Cultures

Tumpat is not just a district; it is a living museum of harmony. Located at the northernmost tip of Kelantan, bordering Thailand, Tumpat is unique for its seamless blend of Malay and Siamese cultures.

From the majestic sleeping buddhas to the serene fishing villages of Pengkalan Kubor, our community thrives on diversity, tolerance, and a deep respect for heritage.

History of Tumpat

Tumpat began as a humble riverside village and has grown into a vibrant community hub in Kelantan. Its unique blend of Siamese and Malay cultures, shaped by centuries of trade and migration, makes Tumpat a living heritage.

Main railway station since the early 20th century, connecting Tumpat to the rest of Malaysia.
Center for trade and tourism, with bustling markets and river ports.
Home to colorful traditional festivals celebrated every year.
