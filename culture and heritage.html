Culture & Heritage of Tumpat

Discover living traditions, festivals and sites where communities celebrate together — curated highlights for visitors and locals.

Explore Highlights Visitor Info
Heritage

Wat Photivihan

Home to the magnificent reclining Buddha and a centre of Thai-Buddhist culture in Tumpat.

Masjid Kampung Laut

Historic wooden mosque with classic Malay timber architecture by the riverside.

Pengkalan Kubor

Coastal border town known for fresh seafood markets and vibrant cross-border trade.

Pesta Wau (Kite Festival)

Annual kite festival celebrating craftsmanship and community spirit on the coast.

Batik & Handicraft

Local artisans preserve batik techniques — find workshops and markets to buy authentic pieces.

Wayang Kulit & Dikir Barat

Traditional performing arts that bring stories and community evenings to life.

Community Stories

Oral histories and portraits: recipes, craft techniques and memories preserved by local families. Read featured stories and find workshop info.

People & Diversity

Malay, Thai/Siamese and Chinese communities live and celebrate together — learn about etiquette, festivals and shared traditions.

Visitor Information

Plan your visit — tips on transport, best times, and respectful behaviour at religious sites. For maps and guided tours, use the links and contact details below.

How to get there

Tumpat is accessible by train from Kota Bharu and by road. Local taxis and motorbike taxis are available for short hops.

Best time to visit

Visit between February and August for dry weather and festivals.
